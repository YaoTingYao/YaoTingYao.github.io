FEMA Emergency Management Data Migration In PostgreSQL

This project proposes to mine a series of Emergency Management and Disaster Datasets provided by the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA). FEMA provides multiple datasets to help reveal the natural hazards, risks, and related emergency response programs throughout the state. The purpose of this project is to create a FEMA Emergency Management dataset containing the information of The National Risk Index (NRI), Community Emergency Response Team (CERT), and Emergency Management Performance Grants (EMPG) datasets to explore if the Emergency Response Team and funding are well allocated against high-risk hazards at the state level.

Cropland Maps on Plane-Scope imagery Using DenseNet and U-Net at National Scales In Zambia

This paper proposes to use DenseNet and U-Net CNNs method to create Cropland Mapping in Republic of Zambia at national scales. We use high spatial resolution Planet-Scope satellite imagery (3-5m) to create image composite for growth and non-growth season from June 2021 to March 2022. We combine Planet-Scope tils into Area of Interests (AOI) clusters based on agro-ecological regions. And then we randomly created field labels within AOI clusters to create validation labels, training reference labels and map reference labels. Use DenseNet and U-Net model to produce a cropland probabilities map for each image pixel and use active learning classifier to filter vectorized field boundaries. Finally, we will access map accuracy.

Assessing Disability Accessible Pedestrian Infrastructure in Manhattan

This project explored the accessibility of pedestrian infrastructure around ADA-compliant subway stations in New York City. We focused specifically on wheelchair user accessibility given the clear set of standards under the ADA Standards for Accessible Design guidance for measuring wheelchair accessibility. The 1-foot resolution digital elevation model (DEM) data were valuable in producing the slope analysis and tabular data containing audits of ramp conditions which are not openly available or have not been produced in other cities. The project assessed sidewalk and pedestrian ramps surrounding accessible subways in the Midtown and Lower Manhattan neighborhoods of NYC. A critical approach was also taken by looking at demographic data on both disability status and income to compare census blocks in Manhattan.
